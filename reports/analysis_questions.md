# Analysis Questions

## Main Business Question
"Is Nike financially healthy, fairly valued, and attractive from an investment perspective?"

## Main Thesis
"Nike may be a turnaround and valuation re-rating opportunity rather than a high-growth stock. Short-term financial performance may remain under pressure due to weak recent revenue growth, margin pressure, channel transition, tariffs, discounts, restructuring costs, and marketing investment. However, long-term upside may appear if Nike can stabilise revenue, recover margins, rebuild wholesale and Amazon channels, improve inventory efficiency, benefit from football and World Cup demand, strengthen its football brand through long-term federation and club partnerships such as the Germany DFB deal, and convert product innovation such as Nike Mind into stronger consumer demand."

This thesis is not a conclusion. It must be tested using verified financial data, stock price data, valuation metrics, official company disclosures, reputable news sources, and reliable market data.

## Evidence Discipline
- Screenshots and user notes are research leads only, not evidence.
- A claim becomes usable evidence only after it is verified through a reliable source.
- All financial, stock, valuation, and operating metrics must be traceable to `data/source_log.csv`.
- Every major conclusion in the final report must be linked to verified data or clearly labelled as an inference.
- Any uncertainty, missing data, outdated information, or conflicting source should be documented.

## Definitions
- Fact: A claim supported by a reliable source.
- Hypothesis: A testable research idea that has not yet been proven.
- Inference: A reasoned interpretation based on verified evidence.
- Conclusion: A final view written only after data collection, validation, and analysis.
